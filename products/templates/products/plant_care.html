Plant Care

How to Water Your Plants

Watering plants may seem simple, but doing it correctly is essential to keeping them healthy and thriving. The first step is to check the soilâ€”stick your finger about one to two inches deep. If it's still moist, wait a day or two. This helps avoid overwatering, one of the most common causes of plant stress and root rot.

The Best Time

Early morning is ideal for watering as plants absorb moisture before the sun heats the soil. Evening watering can leave damp soil overnight, promoting pests and fungi. Always water at the base of the plant, directly onto the soil, so the roots can absorb the moisture. Avoid splashing water on the leaves, especially for indoor plants, as this can encourage mold and disease.
